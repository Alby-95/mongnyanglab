Healthy water intake

Both dogs and cats generally need about 40–60ml per kg of body weight per day. Pets on wet food drink less water because the food is over 70% moisture, which is normal.

When to see a vet

Drinking more than about 100ml per kg per day consistently (excessive thirst) can signal kidney disease, diabetes, or Cushing's. In cats especially, a sudden increase in drinking warrants a checkup. On the other end, cats that barely drink are prone to bladder issues — encourage water with fountains or wet food.

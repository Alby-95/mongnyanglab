The most dangerous things for dogs

Grapes and raisins can cause acute kidney failure and must never be given, regardless of amount. Xylitol — found in sugar-free gum, candy, and even some peanut butter — can cause acute low blood sugar and liver failure even in small amounts, making it an emergency. Onion, garlic, and alcohol are also dangerous beyond small amounts. If ingestion is confirmed, contact a vet immediately even without symptoms, because liver/kidney damage shows up late.

If you catch it early

  • Figure out what and how much they ate (check packaging / leftovers)
  • Do not force vomiting — the vet will treat it safely
  • At night, look up a 24-hour emergency clinic in advance
  • Contact a vet even if there are no symptoms (vomiting, lethargy, tremors, seizures)

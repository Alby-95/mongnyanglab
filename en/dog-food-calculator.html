How the portion is calculated

Vets calculate the Resting Energy Requirement as RER = 70 × body weight (kg) raised to the 0.75 power, then multiply by a factor based on age and spay/neuter status (DER). Dividing that energy by the food's calorie density gives the daily portion in grams.

More important than the number

The calculated value is a starting point. Check weight and rib coverage (body condition score) every two weeks and adjust in 10% steps. On days you give treats, reduce food by the treat calories — treats should stay under 10% of daily energy.

The bag's recommended amount differs. Why?

Bag guidelines often assume an active, intact adult dog, so they can be too high. For spayed/neutered indoor dogs, the calculated value is usually closer to reality.

Where do I find my food's calorie content?

Look for the 'metabolizable energy (ME)' on the back of the bag, usually 330–400 kcal per 100g. If listed per kg, divide by 10.
